# Excel Sales Dashboard – Case Study

## 1. Background

Sales data is one of the most important parts of any business. It helps in understanding performance, identifying trends, and making better decisions.

This project uses Excel to analyze sales data and build a simple dashboard for insights.

---

## 2. Problem

Businesses often want to understand:

* Which products are generating the most revenue?
* Which categories are performing well?
* How does profit compare to sales?
* What trends can be seen over time?

The goal of this project was to answer these questions in a simple and visual way.

---

## 3. Dataset

The dataset includes:

* Product name
* Category and sub-category
* Sales amount
* Profit
* Quantity sold
* Order date

Some cleaning and formatting was done inside Excel.

---

## 4. Approach

### Data preparation

* Removed duplicates
* Fixed formatting issues
* Organized data for pivot tables

### Analysis

Focused on:

* Sales vs profit comparison
* Category and product performance
* Time-based trends
* Top and bottom performers

### Dashboard creation

Built using Excel features like:

* Pivot tables
* Pivot charts
* Slicers for filtering

---

## 5. Findings

### Sales and profit are not always aligned

Some products generate high sales but low profit due to cost or discount effects.

---

### A few products dominate performance

Most of the revenue comes from a small number of products.

---

### Category performance varies

Some categories consistently perform better than others in both sales and profit.

---

### Trends change over time

Sales are not constant and show variation across different time periods.

---

## 6. Business insights

The data shows that focusing only on sales is not enough. Profitability is equally important for business decisions.

Product selection and pricing strategy have a strong impact on overall performance.

---

## 7. Recommendations

* Focus on high-profit products, not just high sales
* Review underperforming categories
* Monitor sales trends regularly
* Improve balance between revenue and profit

---

## 8. Conclusion

This project shows how Excel can be used to analyze business data and create meaningful dashboards without complex tools.

Even simple dashboards can help in making better decisions.

---

## 9. What I learned

* How to build dashboards in Excel
* How to use pivot tables and charts
* How to find business insights from raw data
* How to present data in a clean and understandable way
